Workbench Boundary — What This Tool Is and Is Not

The Workbench is the SA's estimation and packaging engine. It covers everything from Problem Framing through Delivery & Support Model definition. Its output is the structured input package that Practice Leadership uses to draft the formal SOW — it is not the SOW itself.

  • In scope: Pattern selection, complexity calibration, WBS, resource loading, infra cost, assumptions, risks, ROI model, delivery model, executive summary, full export package.
  • Out of scope by design: Diagramming (draw.io, Miro), legal SOW drafting, rate cards and margin, CRM qualification, marketing collateral, eSignature.
  • Partial coverage: Data landscape assessment, integration surface map, RACI matrix, acceptance criteria matrix, constraints log, TCO aggregation — these are captured but not as standalone artefacts.

Critical Handoff Points Where Engagements Break Down

Handoff What Goes Wrong How Workbench Helps
Marketing → SA Win theme gets lost; SA re-discovers client context from scratch Win Theme field in Intake + Overview tab; single source of truth persisted in localStorage
SA → Practice Lead Estimation assumptions not documented; leadership changes scope without adjusting effort Assumptions & Risks tabs with owner and impact fields; exportable for review
SA → Legal Deliverables described technically but not contractually (acceptance criteria are vague) WBS rows include acceptance notes; Delivery tab defines milestone gates and phase approach
SA → Delivery Team SA who estimated is off the project; delivery team inherits undocumented assumptions Full exportable package (Excel, Word, PDF) serves as the handoff document
